The First Settings


You have just installed Ancestris. The default options allow you to immediately start entering your first data. However, it is recommended to quickly get to know some settings to adapt the environment of your software to the way you work and give your genealogy the desired appearance.


This customization can be done in the Preferences menu, that can be accessed from:
  • the menu bar /Tools/Preferences,
  • the ''Preferences'' icon (on the right side of the toolbar),
  • the start window* /My Ancestris/Configure Ancestris.
    (* shown when Ancestris is launched for the first time, then with each launch if the (bottom) box is ticked.)

From there,
  • in the ''Appearance" window:
    • You can choose the language used for the interface.
    • Then the output language used. For example, when you run a report, you will have the output in that language.
    • you can modify the look of the windows (you will have to quit and launch Ancestris again to confirm the change),
    • Do you want Ancestris to remember the opened windows next time you will launch it?
  • in the "Data" window:
    • Which symbols for specific events do you want? This is used for some reports. You should kept the ones already defined.
    •   in the "Text" setting, it is STRONGLY RECOMMENDED NOT TO CHANGE the -246- default setting,
    • Maximum size in kilobytes for images to be displayed. This is important to save memory space. It depends upon your hardware.
    • Which format do you want for names to be displayed?
    • you can choose between 4 different date formats,
    • you can specify in a second tab, your data access preferences in the ''Private data'' tab.
  • in the ''File'' window:
    • You can specify the path to your Gedcom file.
    • Where do you want outputs reports to be stored?
    • The size of the log file. It could be a good idea to increase it to 3200 kb even more because if you have problems and want to report them to the Ancestris developers, they will need your log file.
    • Log level. Do you want the log to be more verbose. The more you increase the informations written in the log file, the slower Ancestris will be. Choose INFO. For testing purpose the developers can ask you to increase it to FINE, even FINEST.
    • Number of backups. Each time you change something in your genealogy (when you save your work), Ancestris creates a backup of your gedcom file, the way it was before your changes. Choose between 5 to 10.
    • Autosave delay in minutes. If you choose 0 autosave is deactivated.
  • in the ''Editing'' window:
    • Add and save spaces between jurisdictions. (if you activate this option, jurisdictions will be shown that way: Springfield, Texas, U.S.A. if you don't, they will be shown that way: Springfield,Texas,U.S.A.).
    • You can choose two ways to enter the jurisdictions (option "Split jurisdictions to edit in Gedcom editor"),

      • or

    • you can increase the maximum number of undo's.
    • you can choose whether, when creating the ancestry of an individual, one or both parents must be created,
    • you can choose to have lastnames displayed in uppercase (recommended to avoid any confusion with names)
    • for the ''Encoding'' option, even if it is not proper Gedcom standard, it is advisable to choose ''UTF-8'' (see Advanced use).

With each option, help is provided by a tooltip.

This is only a selection among all available preference options. Give them a try. You can always get back on your choice.
